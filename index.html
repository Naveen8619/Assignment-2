    Cybersecurity -

    The internet is like the sun, as the internet expands more problems and exploits are prevalent in recent society. But cybersecurity prevents those problems and exploits and includes the “measures used to protect the confidentiality, integrity and availability of systems and information.” (ACSC 2021, Glossary) It is important to be secure online as several threats are prevalent and lurking, these threats include: illegal crypto-mining, data spillage, DoS (Denial of service attacks), Hacking, Identify theft, Malicious insiders, Malware, Phishing, Ransomware, Scams and Web shell malware (ACSC 2021). As technology keeps advancing, new methods of security breaches appear. However, cybersecurity is still seeing an increase in demand and attacks are decreasing, according to Accenture “The total number of cyberattacks dropped 11 percent, from 232 to 206 targeted attacks.” (Accenture 2021, The basics seem better) The decrease of cyberattacks is also shown through “a larger drop of 27 percent in the number of security breaches which indicates the basics seem to be improving”. (Accenture 2021, The basics seem better). Despite this in 2019 “68% of business leaders [still] feel their cybersecurity risks are increasing” (Accenture 2019, New risks from innovation and growth). However Cybersecurity is a risk not limited to businesses, “A survey by the AIC found that identity crime 1 in 4 Australians have been a victim of identity crime at some point in their lives” (Australian Government Department of Home Affairs, 2021) and 68% of the victims have not reported the misuse of their information, this means that 17% of the Australian population has had their personal information misused without reporting it at all this is largely due to the lack of knowledge users have of cybersecurity, the AIC stated that 25.1% of the cases police did not do anything, 12.6% were too embarrassed to report it, 19.3% don’t know where to report it and 12.5% did not know that identify theft was a crime. According to the AIC only 32% of reports were accepted, despite each victim spending an average of 34 hours repairing the damage caused by identify theft. (AIC, 2021). And this is only one of the listed cyber-crimes above.

    And still despite this another huge presence is being developed, quantum computers, quantum computers have the potential to change the entire structure of the internet itself. Quantum computing overpowers classical computers as it utilizes qubits, whereas classic computers only use ones and zeros, qubits have a ‘superposition’ state that allows them to be a one and a zero. Allowing two qubits to represent four scenarios at the same time. (Brandmark, 2021) To put it in perspective Google’s Sycamore quantum processor calculated one million measurements on a quantum circuit, which took around 200s, which a state-of-the-art supercomputer would take 10,000 years to complete. (Physics world 2019, para. 4) Based on the processing speed of the quantum chips, both security and threat wise cybersecurity could be changed immensely. However, quantum computers are still far from becoming a possibility as problems such as quantum decoherence which comes from “aspects of the environment: changing magnetic and electric fields, radiation from warm objects, or cross talk between qubits” (Scientific American 2020, para. 3). Nonetheless IBM (International business Machines corporation) has still promised to develop numerous quantum computers over the next few years coming from the ‘Falcon’ – 27 qubits 2019, ‘Hummingbird’ – 65 qubits 2020, ‘Eagle’ – 127 qubits 2021, ‘Osprey’ – 433 qubits 2022 and ‘Condor’ – 1121 qubits in 2023. (See Figure 1) Figure 1: IBM Roadmap by IBM, 2020 

    A prime example of the importance of cyber security is quantum computing poses as it poses huge impact on cryptography, and in turn affects the crypto mining market heavily. For example, Bitcoin is a system for transferring value, a Bitcoin user is responsible for generating their own random address, and the user’s computer generates a random secret address that is required to transfer Bitcoin. (Deloitte, 2021) However a user with a quantum computer with access to another user’s public key could easily forge the private key and spend their Bitcoin which poses a huge problem for the crypto community. However, Quantum computers are not only threating crypto users but also the encryption and security of everyone on the internet is being threatened especially any type of asymmetric key pairs. Just Like how a quantum computer can crack a Bitcoin users private key, quantum computers can easily brute force and crack a password that uses factorization and prime numbers (havened, 2017) in 1 hour which would take a classic computer 78 million hours to do. It was also estimated that only 120 quantum computers were needed to disrupt every HTTPS connection in 2 seconds in 2017. 

    Cyber-security goes hand in hand with the advancement of technology and as technology keeps advancing so will the jobs related to it. Australia’s cyber security growth network sates that the government has spent 9% more per year and employed 4000 workers per year since 2017. The ASCS found that “90% of Australian organizations faced some form of attempted or successful cyber security compromise” (Seek 2021, para. 1). Cybersecurity affects everything and everyone online ensuring that companies, governments and individual’s information is kept private and secure. 

    Cybersecurity is an important part of everyone’s life, just as people lock their houses and cars to file cabinets and safes, they should lock their phones and information online, almost all information nowadays has an online copy, and an insecure lock could lead to a leak in information. Cybersecurity is prevalent is everyone’s daily lifestyle as users unlock their phones and computers, they also login to websites and applications posing way more information to be kept secret and as more information needs to be secured a large demand in cybersecurity employment for me as an individual is a welcome opportunity as it creates more positions in my ideal job. 

    Cybersecurity also creates an opportunity for more efficient data protection than physical security with thousands of “hidden” locks to be created to protect sensitive data. This affects everyone online as anyone who has access to a computer can encrypt and secure data to prevent access to anyone else. Despite this however many do not protect their online information as much as they should, which is portrayed through the massive identity thieving online. Myself, my family and friends often share passwords to several services and unlike creating copies of a physical key or sharing even more sensitive information so they can access the service, it is much easier and safer to do. Cybersecurity has already become an essential part in modern society and therefore has already affected everyone around me, but the advancement in security will create a more secure, efficient and effective method of information encryption and security. 

    Overall cybersecurity nowadays is impossible to live without, as it would basically be living in a house with no doors and open windows, allowing strangers to pass in and out with no protection at all. And as services such as TLS keep improving to prevent the threats of the advancement of technology such as quantum computers, cybersecurity is an essential part of IT Technology.
